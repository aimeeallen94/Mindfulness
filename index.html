What is Mindfulness?

"Mindfulness is awareness that arises through paying attention, on purpose, in the ,present moment, non-judgementally"
- Jon Kabat-Zinn

Reasons to Practise Mindfuless

Mindfulness promotes neuroplasticity, changing our brains previous neural networks into using more of the grey matter within the brain which is associated with focus, decision-making and memory.
By focusing on the here and now, a pillar of mindfulness practise, mindfulness help reduce rumination and worrying about things that are outside of oour control. By doing this, mindfulness can greatly improve mental health.
Mindfulness has been found to positively impact relationships. Mindfulness encourages an attitude of acceptance and staying present in the moment, this allows us to be fully present when we are with our loved ones and acknowledging that we all have imperfections but accepting these and loving them anyway.
When mindfulness meditation is practised regularly it decreases heart rate and blood pressure as well as increasing serotinin and melatonin. These results not only help our overall general health but greatly reduces the severity of insomnia, helping us to sleep better each night.
